Essay title goes here

Charles J Read · April 2026

First paragraph of the essay. Lead with the claim or the observation. Do not open with "I've been thinking a lot about..." or any other hook-bait preamble. The reader clicked the title — they are already committed. Use the first paragraph to deliver value, not to justify the essay's existence.

Second paragraph continues the argument. Paragraphs should be 2-5 sentences. Long paragraphs are the number one reason readers bounce on mono body text — the uniform visual rhythm fatigues faster than in serif, so shorter paragraphs matter more here than they would in other typefaces.

Use italics for emphasis sparingly. Use bold almost never — maybe once per essay, for a single structural point you want to survive a skim.

Optional subheading

Subheadings are optional. For essays under 1500 words, skip them entirely. For longer essays, use 2-4 maximum. They should name the move the section makes, not summarise its content. "Why the market is wrong about X" beats "Market analysis."

For inline links, write naturally and let the underline do the work. Don't write "click here" or "read more" — name the destination.

Closing paragraph. End on a specific claim, a concrete observation, or a question worth sitting with. Do not end with a call to action, a subscribe prompt, or a summary of what you just said.
